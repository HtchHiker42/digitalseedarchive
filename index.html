    What is LARK?

    LARK (Language Archive and Recovery Kit) is a preservation education project that equips students, teachers, and community members to document endangered and heritage languages. This archive is where that documentation lives.

    Who can submit?

    Any student, educator, or community member who has made a recording with appropriate consent. You don't need to be a linguist. You do need a signed consent form from your speaker.

    What happens next?

    The archive team reviews every bundle. Accepted submissions are assigned a permanent LARK-ID. If the language needs urgent attention, a professional linguist may be connected to your school or community.
